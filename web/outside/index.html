END. x adidas Originals Handball Spezial “Bauhaus” (2023)

Operating between 1919 and 1933, the Staatliches Bauhaus art school focused on a Modernist approach to art, design and architecture with bold typography, block colours and architectural forms, which influence has expanded way beyond its early 20th century introduction.

END. and adidas capture the iconic aesthetic through a trio of classic adidas styles. Whats Bauhaus’ favourite colour? Bright. This version of the Handball Spezial mirrors the movements penchant for clean lined geometric shapes and of course - the distinct colour palette.

Washed in a plush pop of yellow suede, the leather Three Stripes and heel take on the iconic primary colours and layered forms. Further nods sit pride of place at the woven tongue patch.

To finish, END. and the sportswear powerhouse take over the heels - with metallic gold foiled branding, adding adidas’ trademark style.

OUTSIDE SCULPTURE INSPIRED by Oskar Schlemmer (2023)

Oskar Schlemmer was a German painter, sculptor, designer and choreographer associated with the Bauhaus school.

His most famous work is Triadisches Ballett (Triadic Ballet), which saw costumed actors transformed into geometrical representations of the human body in what he described as a "party of form and colour". (Source)

OUTSIDE IS INSPIRED by Frank Lloyd Wright’S FALLINGWATER HOUSE (1935)

After its completion, Time called Fallingwater Wright's "most beautiful job" and it is listed among Smithsonian's "Life List of 28 Places to See Before You Die".

The house was designated a National Historic Landmark on May 11th, 1976. In 1991, members of the American Institute of Architects named Fallingwater the "best all-time work of American architecture" and in 2007, it was ranked 29th on the list of America's Favorite Architecture according to the AIA.
